Before Everything...

I never believed in destiny.

Not really.

I thought the destiny was something that people used for explain the things that couldn't understand. A comfortable lie. A beautiful excuse.

But then it happened.

Now I don't know what to believe anymore.

Some stories begin so quietly that you don't even realize that you are inside one.

This is one of those stories.

How do I explain two people sitting on the same classroom in pre-school as kids... forgetting each other completely for a long time... and finding each other again years later.

In a history class.

Maybe that was the universe trying to tell us something... that we had... it was already history.

This is the story of the secretary and his Madam.

And it began long before either of them knew it had begun.

Chapter One – The Forgotten Classroom

Once upon a time.

There was a classroom called Lotus.

I know that now.

But I forgot about it for years.

The desks. The faces. The walls.

All forgotten.

But she was there, with bright eyes, and a warmer smile.

Sitting somewhere in that classroom.

Living in her childhood. Having no idea that the boy in the classroom would one day lose his sleep over her.

And I was there too.

Just a kid.

Breathing the same air as someone who would one day changed everything inside me without even trying.

The same classroom. Same teacher. Same world.

And we both forgot.

They may have laughed together once.

They may have shared a pencil or borrowed an eraser.

Small moments... forgettable moments.

Or so they thought.

Because what we didn't know, both of them couldn't possibly understand at that age was that the universe was already writing their story.

Quietly. Patiently. Even without asking their permission.

The universe had planted a seed in the little Lotus classroom.

And then waited.

And they drifted apart without even saying goodbye.

Chapter Two – Time The Thief

Time passed the way it always does.

Without any mercy. Silently. Without warning.

The childhood became a collection of blurry images and faded the laughter.

The Lotus classroom became a distant memory.

She grew up. He grew up.

Life pulled them in different ways the way life always does.

Years went by.

He forgot her name.

She forgot his face.

And the seed that the universe had planted in that little classroom lay down under the soil of time, waiting for the right moment to grow.

I didn't think about that classroom.

I didn't think about the girl who sat somewhere in it.

How could I? I didn't even remember her name at least.

But the universe remembered!

The universe knew something we didn't.

It never tells you what it's planning!!

Chapter Three – History Class

Nobody told me it would happened in history class.

Just another ordinary day.

Just another ordinary class.

I walked in, sat down, opened my book, keep doing my own works.

And then someone came in...

She walked in with her friend.

Something happened inside me that I still can't explain properly.

It wasn't dramatic. No music. No lightning.

It was quieter than that.

Like a memory trying to come back from somewhere very deep.

"I know her"

That's what my mind was wandering.

I looked at her.

And she looked at me.

Just for a moment.

Something that had been sleeping for years suddenly opened its eyes.

Quietly. Permanently.

That morning during the interval, I found mangoes.

There was a tree just outside the classroom.

We grabbed a few, got together and ate.

I didn't think anything of it.

But she noticed.

That evening she walked into history class with mangoes.

Properly prepared this time.

Salt. Chilli. Sugar.

They were absolutely delicious!

I ate them without even thinking.

She watched me eating and smiled.

"I brought it because of him," she said so casually.

It's something about the way she stayed with me.

She had noticed me.

She had done something small for me.

The sweetest moments never warn you. ❤️‍🩹
